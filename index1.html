Simple Omlette Recipe

To find The Nutrition Information, The Recipe, The Ingredients, and The Preparation please use the navigation bar on the left hand side.

h

About

Preparation

  • Total: Approximately 10 minutes
  • Preparation: 5 minutes
  • Cooking: 5 minutes

About

Ingredients

  • 2-3 large eggs
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of butter or oil
  • Optional fillings: cheese, diced vegetables, cooked meats, herbs

About

Instructions

  1. Beat the eggs: In a bowl, beat the eggs with a pinch of salt and pepper until they are well mixed. You can add a tablespoon of water or milk for a fluffier texture.

  2. Heat the pan: Place a non-stick frying pan over medium heat and add butter or oil.

  3. Cook the omelette: Once the butter is melted and bubbling, pour in the eggs. Tilt the pan to ensure the eggs evenly coat the surface.

  4. fillings (optional): When the eggs begin to set at the edges but are still slightly runny in the middle, sprinkle your chosen fillings over one half of the omelette.

  5. Fold and serve: As the omelette continues to cook, carefully lift one edge and fold it over the fillings. Let it cook for another minute, then slide it onto a plate.

  6. Enjoy: Serve hot, with additional salt and pepper if needed.

Evaluation

  • In my opinion, taking digital tech for year 10 was an excellent choice. I have really enjoyed it and I would strongly reccomend it to anyone looking to persue their love for computers. The parts I personally enjoyed the most are:

  • Having a space to express my creativity.

  • Working on large projects with plenty of time, and not feeling pressured by the amount of time we had.

  • Being able to colaborate with my friends.

  • Being able to use what I have learnt in class to do projects of my own at home.

  • The biggest challanges I faced during these projects were, learning to use the programs. It was my first time using them. Learning javascript, it was quite the step up from html. I overcame this by doing a lot of practice, watching videos to educate myself and doing tasks set by my teacher. Lastly, finding the correct colour schemes for my pages. I overcame this by using the generate colour scheme feature on adobe colour, as well as looking around for websites I liked the design of. Separate from this course at home with my Rasperry Pi I strugled a lot with downloading the correct functions to my console, so that my code would work properly with my GPIO pins. I overcame this by watching tutorials and using the internet to find out what needed to be done.

  • While doing this course I developed many useful skills. The first of which is; the ability to replicate a page based on an image of it. Done with the replication page in term 1 with Web Dev. I found this skill to be very usefull as I can now look at a website and tell what code I should write to make mine look similar. Using easing functions with javascript to make my animations look better. I found this skill very useful in the Javascript section where I needed to have my timing just right so my conveyor belt would look like it's actually moving the objects along it.
